Celebrating a quarter century of Champions League football


Created in 1992 as a re-brand for the old European Cup, the Champions League has been the elite competition in continental football for nearly 25 years, and is among the most compelling annual sport on show. Players from all over the world sign just for the chance to turn out in a Champions League game. Such is the strength of the sides on show that no team has retained the title in consecutive seasons. Clubs from all major European divisions have won the Champions League, beginning with Marseille from France's Ligue 1 in 1993. It's sides from Spain, namely La Liga's El Clasico rivals Real Madrid and Barcelona that have been the most successful in recent seasons, with six of the last 11 wins between them. From a Premier League perspective, Manchester United, Liverpool and Chelsea have all reached the pinnacle of European football, while Arsenal were beaten finalists in 2006. The Champions League has been the making of some managers; just ask Jose Mourinho, who took unfancied Porto all the way in 2004 and then switched to Stamford Bridge before going on to manage other massive clubs like Inter Milan, Real Madrid and Man Utd. German Bundesliga heavyweights Bayern Munich and Borussia Dortmund have both tasted Champions League glory before, alongside Dutch Eredivisie giants Ajax. Juventus and AC Milan complete the modern era roll of honour in this competition, joining fellow Serie A side Inter.
